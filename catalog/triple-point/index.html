About this work

Sarah Sze's Triple Point (Pendulum) transformed the US Pavilion at the 2013 Venice Biennale into an immersive laboratory of interconnected material flows. The title references the thermodynamic triple point of water, where solid, liquid, and gas coexist simultaneously, and the installation embodies this threshold state through a sprawling constellation of everyday objects, photographs, lights, and technological fragments suspended in precarious equilibrium. A swinging pendulum traces arcs through the assembled materials, defining the work's boundaries while threatening its destruction. Sze collected elements from Venice itself during a three-month installation period, incorporating vaporetto tickets, leaves from the Giardini, and photographs of local stone into the growing system. Each room of the pavilion functioned as an experimental site where humble objects aspired to become instruments of cosmic measurement. The work epitomizes Sze's practice of building intricate ecosystems from detritus, where the viewer navigates flows of information and matter that oscillate between order and entropy, model and ruin, the intimately personal and the astronomically vast.

Catalog Relevance

Demonstrates how modularity and systems thinking operate through accumulation of everyday materials rather than industrial components, creating spatial complexity from found-object networks that parallel aluminum's systemic presence in infrastructure.
