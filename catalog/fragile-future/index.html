About this work

Studio Drift's Fragile Future is a painstaking meditation on the tension between natural fragility and technological permanence. Each module consists of a bronze-cast three-dimensional circuit board fitted with an LED, onto which real dandelion seeds are individually glued by hand. The work operates at the intersection of industrial production and impossible delicacy, its modular structure allowing configurations to scale from intimate tabletop pieces to vast architectural chandeliers. The dandelion, nature's most efficient seed-dispersal mechanism, is frozen in a state of perpetual almost-flight, tethered to the electronic grid that powers its illumination. This juxtaposition generates a poignant visual metaphor for the precarious relationship between organic systems and technological networks. The bronze circuits reference the history of printed circuit boards while elevating them from hidden infrastructure to visible ornament. Each module requires hours of hand labor, a deliberate rejection of the mass-production logic embedded in the circuit board itself. Fragile Future thus embodies its own contradiction: a handmade object that mimics industrial seriality, a natural specimen preserved through technological intervention, a light source powered by the very systems threatening the ecosystems it memorializes.

Catalog Relevance

Merges biological material with electronic infrastructure at the scale of craft, creating a poetic counterpoint to industrial fabrication methods and raising questions about technology's relationship to the natural world.
