About this work

Sterling Ruby's Soft Sculpture series produces oversized, bulbous fabric forms stuffed with fiber fill and saturated with industrial dyes, creating objects that hover between the traditions of craft, industrial production, and fine art sculpture. The works take various configurations including 'husbands,' modeled on commercially sold pillow shapes, and prone figures resembling collapsed human bodies, sewn from materials including denim, prison uniforms, and repurposed institutional textiles. Ruby's dyeing process involves pouring and spraying industrial pigments across the fabric surfaces, producing bleeding, dripping chromatic effects that recall both Abstract Expressionist painting and the staining of used industrial materials. The sculptures' exaggerated scale and sagging, corporeal presence evoke bodies subjected to institutional systems of control, a theme Ruby has explored through his sustained interest in carceral aesthetics and subcultural resistance. By combining textile craft techniques traditionally coded as domestic and feminine with industrial-scale production and aggressive material processes, Ruby collapses hierarchies between high art, craft, and industrial manufacturing. The resulting forms are simultaneously seductive and disturbing, their vivid surfaces concealing dense foam interiors that give them an unsettling bodily weight and presence in the gallery space.
