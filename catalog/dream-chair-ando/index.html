About this work

Tadao Ando's Dream Chair represents the architect's first foray into furniture design, translating his signature sensitivity to material and geometric precision from concrete buildings to solid wood. The chair's continuously curving shell emerges from a single sheet of three-dimensionally bent ash, a technical achievement requiring Carl Hansen's most advanced wood-bending capabilities. Ando's architectural practice has always privileged the expressive potential of raw materials, whether exposed concrete, water, or light, and the Dream Chair extends this philosophy to wood grain and timber mass. The form suggests a gentle embrace, its sweeping curves creating a cocoon-like enclosure that echoes the contemplative interiors of Ando's chapels and meditation spaces. Unlike his buildings, which achieve emotional power through austere geometry, the chair introduces an unusual softness into Ando's vocabulary, as if the warmth of wood unlocked a tenderness his concrete resists. The manufacturing process pushes wood beyond its natural bending limits through steam and pressure, forcing an inherently fibrous, directional material into compound curves that appear effortless but required years of prototyping to perfect.
