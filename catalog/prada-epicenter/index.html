About this work

OMA's Prada Epicenter in SoHo reimagined the luxury retail store as a laboratory for architectural ideas, collapsing distinctions between shopping, cultural programming, and public space. The centerpiece is a sweeping wooden wave that descends from street level to the basement, functioning simultaneously as display surface, seating, stage, and circulation. Aluminum and polycarbonate panels create walls that shift between transparent and opaque, while movable fixtures allow the space to transform from shop to gallery to lecture hall. Koolhaas explicitly rejected conventional retail design, instead treating the commission as an opportunity to test ideas about flexibility, spectacle, and the commodification of experience that had animated his theoretical work for decades. The project draws directly from Cedric Price's Fun Palace concept, translating that unrealized vision of infinitely adaptable architecture into the unlikely context of luxury fashion. The Epicenter's material palette of industrial aluminum, translucent polycarbonate, and raw infrastructure deliberately undermines luxury retail expectations, creating productive friction between the preciousness of the merchandise and the rawness of its architectural container.

Catalog Relevance

Uses aluminum and industrial materials to critique luxury retail conventions, demonstrating how architectural material choices carry ideological weight and how commercial commissions can serve as platforms for spatial experimentation.
