About this work

Dieter Rams's 606 Universal Shelving System is the definitive expression of modular design as ethical practice. Conceived in 1960 and continuously produced by Vitsœ ever since, it consists of aluminum E-profile tracks that mount to walls, accepting an interchangeable array of shelves, cabinets, and desk surfaces. The system's genius lies in its refusal to become obsolete: components purchased decades apart remain fully compatible, and the entire assembly can be reconfigured, expanded, or relocated without waste. The aluminum extrusion is not decorative but structural, its E-shaped cross-section engineered to grip shelf pins at precise intervals while distributing load evenly across the wall plane. Rams designed it during his tenure at Braun, where his ten principles of good design crystallized into doctrine. The 606 embodies all ten, but especially the tenth—as little design as possible. Every element exists for a reason; nothing is ornamental. The system has outlived trends, movements, and its designer's own active career, functioning as living proof that industrial materials and rigorous systems thinking can produce objects of genuine permanence.

Catalog Relevance

The 606 is perhaps the most elegant demonstration of aluminum extrusion as a design language—its E-profile tracks are simultaneously structural engineering and visual grammar, proving that industrial process can generate enduring beauty.
