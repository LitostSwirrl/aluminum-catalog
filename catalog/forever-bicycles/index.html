About this work

Ai Weiwei's Forever Bicycles assembles over 1,200 steel-framed bicycles from Shanghai's Forever brand into a monumental lattice structure that transforms one of China's most ubiquitous manufactured objects into architectural spectacle. The bicycles are stripped of seats and handlebars -- rendered non-functional -- and interconnected through their frames to create a permeable, labyrinthine wall whose visual density shifts dramatically with the viewer's position. From certain angles the precisely aligned frames produce a moiré-like optical effect; from others, the installation dissolves into a thicket of wheels, spokes, and tubular steel. The Forever brand name operates as bitter irony: these symbols of Chinese modernity and individual mobility are arrested, locked into collective immobility. First conceived for the Taipei Fine Arts Museum in 2011 and subsequently reinstalled at increasing scales across international venues -- sometimes exceeding 3,000 units -- the work extends Ai's ongoing investigation into how mass production erases individuality while generating collective power. The bicycle, once China's primary vehicle of personal freedom, becomes a monument to the tension between the individual and the state, mobility and confinement, the handmade and the industrially replicated.

Catalog Relevance

The work's transformation of mass-manufactured metal objects into monumental sculptural form through sheer accumulation parallels strategies of aluminum seriality, where identical extruded units gain new meaning through repetition and reconfiguration.
