About this work

Hydro's CIRCAL R100 is the first commercially available aluminum product made entirely from 100% post-consumer recycled scrap, achieving a carbon footprint below 0.5 kg CO2 equivalent per kilogram, roughly 97% lower than the global average for primary aluminum production. Launched as a development of Hydro's broader CIRCAL line, which guarantees minimum 75% post-consumer content verified by third-party auditor DNV, the R100 product represents the theoretical endpoint of aluminum's circular material biography. Its feedstock comprises discarded bicycle frames, chair legs, window profiles, shower enclosures, car bumpers, and other post-consumer artifacts, each carrying embedded histories of use, wear, and obsolescence that are melted down and reformed into new extrusion profiles. The production process is both technically demanding and deliberately limited: sorting, cleaning, and remelting post-consumer scrap without virgin aluminum requires precise alloy management to maintain structural properties. Currently available only in small on-demand batches, CIRCAL R100 exists at the intersection of industrial innovation and material philosophy, demonstrating that aluminum can theoretically cycle indefinitely without degradation, embodying a material whose biography is perpetually rewritten rather than concluded.

Catalog Relevance

Directly embodies the concept of material biography as applied to aluminum, demonstrating that the metal's infinite recyclability creates a traceable lifecycle connecting post-consumer artifacts to new industrial products without material degradation.
