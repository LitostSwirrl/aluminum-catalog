About this work

Cornelia Parker's Cold Dark Matter: An Exploded View suspends the charred and fragmented remains of a garden shed, blown up by the British Army's School of Ammunition, around a single light bulb in a darkened gallery. The fragments — twisted metal hardware, splintered wood, shattered glass, warped tools — hang frozen in mid-detonation on nearly invisible wires, casting dramatic shadows on the surrounding walls that double the installation's visual presence. Parker filled the shed before its destruction with the accumulated detritus of domestic life: tools, toys, books, and garden implements, ensuring the explosion would produce a rich archaeology of everyday material culture. The work transforms an act of violent destruction into suspended contemplation, making visible the moment of transition between intact object and scattered debris. The metal components — hinges, nails, brackets, springs — undergo radical material transformation through the explosion, revealing industrial hardware's latent sculptural potential when freed from functional contexts. Parker's practice of subjecting found objects to extreme processes (crushing, stretching, exploding) creates a poetics of material transformation that exposes hidden properties and associations within mundane industrial and domestic objects.

Catalog Relevance

Reveals the sculptural and poetic potential of industrial metal hardware when violently liberated from functional domestic contexts, transforming mundane materials into suspended meditations on destruction and material transformation.
