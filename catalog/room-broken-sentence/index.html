About this work

Mark Manders' Room with Broken Sentence is a perpetual installation within his lifelong project Self-Portrait as a Building, an evolving conceptual architecture begun in 1986 that treats his entire oeuvre as rooms within a fictional edifice. The work combines figurative clay sculptures, often depicting partially formed or arrested human heads and figures, with architectural elements of painted steel, rope, and wood that define and bisect the space. Nothing is what it seems in Manders' material world: epoxy masquerades as clay, clay becomes bronze, and bronze appears to be wood, creating a temporal vertigo where objects seem simultaneously ancient and freshly made. The figures appear caught mid-formation, as if the process of sculpting has been frozen at a precise moment, their surfaces bearing the marks of hands that seem to have just departed. Presented at the 2013 Venice Biennale in Gerrit Rietveld's Dutch Pavilion, the installation entered into dialogue with modernist architecture, creating charged spatial environments where minimal linear elements and monumental figurative presences coexist in suspended narrative. The broken sentence of the title suggests meaning perpetually interrupted, never arriving at completion.

Catalog Relevance

Explores seriality through the ongoing Self-Portrait as a Building project, where repeated formal elements and material deceptions create a systematic practice that parallels the serial logic of industrial production and aluminum extrusion.
