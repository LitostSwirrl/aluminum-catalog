About this work

Faye Toogood's 'Roly-Poly Chair' is a deliberately chunky, totemic seating object composed of rounded cylindrical volumes stacked and joined into a squat, anthropomorphic form. Originally produced in fiberglass and later cast in aluminum and bronze, the chair rejects the slender, refined proportions that dominate contemporary furniture in favor of an almost prehistoric solidity. Toogood, who trained as an art historian before becoming a designer, brings a sculptor's sensibility to furniture: the Roly-Poly's bulbous limbs suggest a seated figure as much as a chair, blurring the boundary between object and body. The piece emerged from Toogood's 'Assemblage 2' collection, which explored primitive archetypes and elemental forms. Its material versatility—moving from fiberglass to cast aluminum—demonstrates how a single form can shift register depending on surface and weight. The aluminum version gains gravitas and thermal presence that the lighter fiberglass lacks. The Roly-Poly has become an emblem of the collectible design movement, where furniture operates at the intersection of art market speculation and functional object, challenging both categories simultaneously.

Catalog Relevance

Illustrates how cast aluminum can transform a sculptural furniture concept, adding material weight and thermal character that shifts the object from playful prototype to collectible art piece.
