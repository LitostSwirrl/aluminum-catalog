About this work

Seeing Glass distills Sabine Marcelis's ongoing investigation into the phenomenology of industrial materials—how they absorb, transmit, and transform light. Each piece pairs a circular disc of cast resin, saturated with gradients of translucent color, with a machined aluminum frame that serves as both structural armature and optical boundary. The aluminum edge is not merely functional; its milled precision and reflective surface create a liminal zone where industrial metal meets chromatic depth. Marcelis trained as an industrial designer, and her material sensibility resists the decorative: the resin is not applied as finish but cast as volume, so color becomes a spatial experience rather than a surface treatment. The aluminum component insists on the object's manufactured origin, preventing the resin from reading as purely organic or mystical. Exhibited at Galleria Nilufar during Milan Design Week, Seeing Glass positioned itself at the contested boundary between design and art, leveraging aluminum's industrial neutrality to anchor objects that behave more like paintings than furniture.

Catalog Relevance

Seeing Glass demonstrates aluminum's role as a precise, industrially neutral frame that allows other materials to perform optically—revealing how extrusion and machining can serve expressive rather than purely structural ends.
