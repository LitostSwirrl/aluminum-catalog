About this work

Concrete Objects is Samuel Ross's ongoing design project that produces functional objects and furniture from deliberately raw industrial materials: poured concrete, extruded aluminum, vulcanized rubber. Each piece retains the unfinished quality of construction-site materials, their surfaces bearing the marks of molds, formwork, and industrial process. Ross, who trained under Virgil Abloh and founded the streetwear label A-COLD-WALL*, brings fashion's understanding of material signification to product design, recognizing that concrete and raw metal carry social meaning beyond their physical properties. The objects explore how industrial materials encode class and racial identity, how the brutalist aesthetic of social housing projects becomes a visual language associated with specific communities. A concrete bench is never merely a seating surface; it evokes the public infrastructure of estates, bus stops, and barriers designed to control bodies in urban space. Ross's aluminum components are left with visible extrusion marks and machining evidence, refusing the polished finish that would elevate them into conventional luxury goods. The project insists that industrial materials possess a social biography that design cannot neutralize, only acknowledge or suppress.

Catalog Relevance

Directly engages aluminum extrusion as a material carrying class and racial signification, refusing the polished finish that typically strips industrial metals of their social context and insisting on the visibility of manufacturing process as political statement.
