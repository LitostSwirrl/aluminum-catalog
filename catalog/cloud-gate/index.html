About this work

Anish Kapoor's Cloud Gate is a 110-ton elliptical sculpture fabricated from 168 polished stainless steel plates, seamlessly welded and polished to an impossibly smooth mirror finish that reflects and distorts the Chicago skyline, clouds, and visitors in its curved surface. Colloquially known as 'The Bean,' the work required unprecedented fabrication techniques developed in collaboration with performance engineering firm MTI, pushing industrial metalworking capabilities to achieve Kapoor's vision of a boundaryless reflective object. The sculpture's twelve-foot-high arch, called the omphalos, creates a concave chamber underneath where reflections multiply and converge. Cloud Gate operates at the intersection of industrial engineering and phenomenological experience: its stainless steel surface must be regularly maintained to preserve the optical illusion of a liquid mercury form, demanding ongoing industrial labor to sustain its apparently effortless presence. Kapoor's insistence on eliminating all visible seams required years of grinding and polishing beyond standard architectural metalwork practice. The work demonstrates how advanced industrial steel fabrication can produce objects that transcend their material origins, creating experiences of visual dematerialization through the very precision of their engineering.
