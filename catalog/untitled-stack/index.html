About this work

Judd's Untitled (Stack) consists of twelve identical rectangular units mounted vertically on a wall, each separated by equal intervals. The work exemplifies Judd's rejection of compositional hierarchy — no element is more important than any other, and the spaces between units are as significant as the units themselves. The stack operates as a system: its logic is immediately apprehensible, yet its physical presence exceeds its conceptual simplicity. Judd insisted on industrial fabrication, using sheet metal shops and later aluminum fabricators to achieve the precision and impersonality he demanded. The stack format became one of Judd's most enduring structures, repeated across materials including stainless steel, aluminum, copper, and Plexiglas. Each material inflects the same form differently — the galvanized iron of this early version has a matte, utilitarian quality that emphasizes the industrial origin of both form and fabrication. The stack is fundamentally modular: it could theoretically extend infinitely, bounded only by the wall. This open-endedness distinguishes it from traditional sculpture's self-contained forms. Judd's stacks anticipate the logic of extrusion — identical cross-sections repeated along an axis — though here the axis is vertical and the repetition discrete rather than continuous.

Catalog Relevance

The stack is extrusion logic made explicit: identical units repeated along an axis. Judd's insistence on industrial fabrication and his treatment of material as non-expressive mirrors the extrusion process itself.
